## 3. How to Ask Claw for Help

### Tips for Best Results

1. **Be specific about the platform**
   - ❌ *"Write a post"*
   - ✅ *"Write a Facebook post for our business page about emergency lockout services"*

2. **Specify the audience**
   - ❌ *"Create a recruitment post"*
   - ✅ *"Create a recruitment post targeting experienced auto locksmiths in London"*

3. **Give context on what's working**
   - ✅ *"Our post about the Digital Paper Trail got the most engagement. Create 3 more posts exploring that theme"*

4. **Ask for multiple options**
   - ✅ *"Give me 3 different versions of a post about transparent pricing — one emotional, one factual, one with a question hook"*

5. **Reference the files**
   - ✅ *"Look at our content calendar and tell me what topics we should add for the next 30 posts"*
   - ✅ *"Based on our recruitment posts, what messaging angle haven't we tried yet?"*

## 7. Pro Tips

### 💡 Getting More From Claw

1. **Build on previous conversations** — Claw remembers context within a session. Start each session with a brief update: *"Since we last spoke, our recruitment post in the locksmith group got 15 likes and 3 comments"*

2. **Ask for batches** — Instead of one post at a time, ask for 5–10 posts. You'll have content ready to go.

3. **Request different formats** — *"Give me this as a Facebook post, a Twitter post, and a LinkedIn post"*

4. **Use Claw as a sounding board** — *"I'm thinking of running a promotion where new locksmiths get their first month free. How should we announce this on social media?"*

5. **Keep Claw updated** — When something changes (new service, new city coverage, pricing update), tell Claw so it can adjust its content accordingly.
