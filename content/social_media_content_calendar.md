### 📘 Facebook
🔑 Know Your Locks: A Quick Guide for UK Homeowners

🔒 Yale Latch Lock — The spring-loaded lock that closes behind you. Common, but NOT secure on its own.

🔐 Deadbolt/Mortice Lock — The one you turn with a key. Much more secure. Insurance companies often require a 5-lever mortice.

🔓 Euro Cylinder — Found in uPVC doors. Vulnerable to 'lock snapping' if not anti-snap rated.

🔏 Smart Lock — Keyless entry via phone/code. Convenient, but check the backup options!

💡 Top tip: Most insurance policies require a BS3621-rated lock. Not sure what you have? Ask LockSafe!

📞 07818 333 989

---

### 💼 LinkedIn
Understanding lock types is fundamental to effective property security. A brief overview for property professionals:

• Yale/Latch Locks: Spring-loaded, auto-closing. Insufficient as sole security.
• Mortice Deadlocks: Key-operated, significantly more secure. 5-lever BS3621-rated models are typically required for insurance compliance.
• Euro Cylinders: Standard in uPVC doors. Must be anti-snap rated to resist forced entry.
• Smart Locks: Keyless convenience with digital access. Ensure backup entry methods exist.
